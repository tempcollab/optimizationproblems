### Headline correction to the prior memory (load-bearing, freshly verified this round)
The run_state Rule (R3) and math-explorer memory assert "~0.19 of the slack is the MIN-REDUCTION gap; ZZ-minimal roots sit OFF both circles on the lemniscate |z(1-z)|=const where mean_nu f ~0.44 >> 0.249." **This is WRONG for the actual extremal object and I corrected it by direct computation.** Taking the near-extremal ZZ polynomial (Flammang Table-1 j=15, deg 16 in X=z(1-z), the smallest-height ZZ poly, true h_Z = 0.25272):
- All 32 conjugate roots z lie within **0.0299** of one of the two circles |z|=1 or |1-z|=1 (mean distance **0.0127**). They are NOT off on a far lemniscate; they hug the circles.
- mean_nu sigma_ZZ = **0.25272** (= the poly's true h_Z), NOT 0.44. The "0.44" figure does not reproduce from either the honest density sigma_ZZ (0.25272) or the trap density log+|w| (0.2419).
- Flammang's f0 has its global 2-D PLANE min ON the circle (verified: plane-min argz has |z|=0.998, plane-min = circle-min). So the min-locus reduction `sum f(alpha_i) >= d*min_PLANE f` reduces to |z|=1 with **zero loss** — it is tight, not the leak.

**The true slack is NOT a wrong-locus / off-circle leak. It is the gap between the circle MIN of f (0.2487458) and the MEAN of f over the near-circle conjugate measure (0.25272)** — a min-vs-mean gap of ~0.004, with the conjugate measure essentially supported on (a neighborhood of) the two circles. This reframes the whole problem and partially un-blocks question 1.

### Where the slack is, and the verdict on the three requested mechanisms

**Q1 — Containment / root-location lemma to replace d*min_PLANE f with something larger.**
- The premise is now SHARPER and BETTER than memory said: the conjugate roots are NOT spread on a far lemniscate; they cluster within 0.03 of the two circles. So a containment lemma is plausible in PRINCIPLE — but it does not help the way hoped, because **Flammang's plane-min is already on the circle**, so restricting the locus to a band around the circles cannot raise min_PLANE f above the circle min (the min is IN the band). Containment buys nothing for the min-reduction as currently structured.
- The REAL opening is different and is the softest target this round: replace the crude `sum_i f(alpha_i) >= d * min f` by a **mean estimate** that exploits that the conjugate measure cannot concentrate at the single circle-min point t* (the binding lobe). The conjugates equidistribute (Bilu/Doche Doc01a Thm 2 gives the limiting root distribution as an explicit equidistribution of the perturbed family on a curve in the plane), so `(1/d) sum f(alpha_i)` is an INTEGRAL of f against a near-equilibrium measure, which is strictly above min f. The hard step: turning "the conjugates equidistribute, so the mean exceeds the min" into a RIGOROUS lower bound valid for ALL ZZ-minimal polys (not just the limit) — i.e. an effective equidistribution / discrepancy bound for conjugates of small-height numbers. This is genuinely new machinery for this constant and is NOT the energy column. It is a research longshot (effective equidistribution with explicit constants is hard), but it attacks the CORRECT slack (min-vs-mean), unlike everything tried R1-R3.

**Q2 — Schur-Siegel-Smyth power-sum / multivariate LP (the named "softer target").**
- Verdict: **BARRED — it is the same multivariate-integrality family already killed for ZZ.** OSS's actual new column is `log|Q(x,y)|`, Q in Z[x,y], integrated against dmu(x)dmu(y) (eq 3) — power-sum/Newton-identity moments are just symmetric special cases of this. For ZZ the column needs `prod_{i,j} Q(alpha_i, alpha_j)` to be a nonzero integer, but `prod_{i,j} Q(alpha_i,alpha_j) = Res-type / a_lead^k`, which is < 1 for non-integer alpha (verified in memory: Q=xy-1 gives -0.0933<0 for P=10x^2-6x+1; the energy column Q=x-y is the I(nu)<0 instance). OSS's two hypotheses that make this valid — alpha a totally-positive ALGEBRAIC INTEGER (a_lead=1) and nu on a REAL INTERVAL — are BOTH absent for ZZ (alpha ranges over all algebraic numbers, nu is complex on the two circles). The OSS support-containment lemma (Lemma 2.6) ALSO depends on the energy condition I(mu)>=0 (the truncation takes a convex combination with an equilibrium measure precisely to preserve I(mu')>=0), so it does not transfer energy-free either. **Do not propose any power-sum / multivariate / two-variable-resultant LP — it reduces to the barred integer-locus reduction.**

**Q3 — Genuinely different machinery from analogous constants.**
- Surveyed exhaustively in R3 (R3_borrow_survey.md): every lower-bound machine across SSS-trace, Mahler/Lehmer, house/Schinzel-Zassenhaus, Faltings ess-min [BMRL], and ZZ is Smyth's auxiliary-function / integer-transfinite-diameter LP. The ONLY published post-LP advance is OSS's multivariate column — barred. SOS/SDP has NEVER beaten the LP for any of these (it only recovers the continuum LP optimum; adds no arithmetic content). No new borrowable lower-bound mechanism exists. CONFIRMED this round; nothing to add.
- The one orthogonal idea NOT in that family is the effective-equidistribution / discrepancy route in Q1 above — it comes from Bilu equidistribution + Doche Doc01a Thm 2's explicit limit measure, not from the LP literature.

### Angles to try (ranked, honest)
1. **(Longshot, correct target) Min-vs-mean via effective equidistribution.** Prove a rigorous `(1/d) sum_i f(alpha_i) >= min f + delta` for ZZ-minimal polys by bounding the discrepancy of the conjugates from equilibrium (the conjugates cannot all sit at the single binding lobe t*~0.49). Hard step: an EFFECTIVE equidistribution bound with an explicit positive constant delta > 1.5e-4, valid for ALL ZZ-minimal polys of large degree, plus handling small degrees by finite check. This is the only angle attacking the genuine slack. Research-grade, not cheap.
2. **(Diagnostic, cheap, likely negative) Quantify the min-vs-mean gap numerically** across the known small-height ZZ polynomials (Doche/Flammang tables) to see whether delta is bounded below by a usable constant empirically — a cheap gating computation before any build. If the per-poly (mean f - min f) shrinks toward 0 as degree grows, angle 1 is dead; if it stays >~0.003, angle 1 is worth the hard equidistribution work.
3. **(Off-focus) Upper side** — held 0.2540419719 (R11), pushable, but the user's standing focus is the lower bound; do not switch without a fresh user signal.
