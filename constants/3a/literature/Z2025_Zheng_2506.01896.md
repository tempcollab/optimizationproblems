# Z2025 — Zheng, "Sums and differences of sets: a further improvement over AlphaEvolve" arXiv:2506.01896

PDF: `pdfs/2506.01896.pdf` (+ `.txt`). Result: $C_{3a}\ge 1.173077$ (limit of Gerbicz family).

## What it does
Takes Gerbicz's $W(m,L,B)$ + base-$(2B+1)$ construction and computes the **$m\to\infty$
asymptotic limit** of $\theta$, replacing the finite exact search by a clean variational
formula. Confirms the uniform-digit family is essentially saturated at ~1.1731.

## Large-deviation lemma (the engine)
With $L=\lfloor rm\rfloor$ and each $x_i$ uniform on $\{0,\dots,B\}$ (mean $B/2$):
$$ \lim_{m\to\infty}\frac{\log|W(m,\lfloor rm\rfloor,B)|}{m}=\log(B+1)-I(r,B), $$
$$ I(c,B)=\begin{cases}0,& c\ge B/2\\[2pt]
\sup_t\!\big(tc-\log\frac{1+e^t+\dots+e^{Bt}}{B+1}\big),& c<B/2.\end{cases} $$
$I(\cdot,B)$ is the Cramér rate function (Legendre transform of the uniform-$\{0..B\}$ mgf).

## The closed-form exponent (set $L=\lfloor rm\rfloor$, $k=\lfloor aL\rfloor$ in $d(U)$)
$$\theta\ \ge\
\frac{\log 2+ar\log B+(1-ar)\log(B+1)-I(ar,1)-ar\,I(\tfrac{1-a}{a},B-1)-(1-ar)I(\tfrac{r}{1-ar},B)+I(2r,2B)}{\log(2B+1)}.$$
- numerator term $\log(2B+1)-I(2r,2B)$ = $\log s/m$ (sumset); $\log q/m\le\log(2B+1)$.
- The $d(U)$ lower bound uses a SINGLE term $k=\lfloor aL\rfloor$ of the sum (2.5) — **crude**,
  flagged by Zheng as improvable.

## Open / improvable (named by author) — THE LEVERS
1. **Continuous $B$** instead of integer grid $\{3..10\}$.
2. **Tighter $d(U)$ analysis** — currently a crude single-$k$ lower bound; the full sum (2.5)
   is larger.
3. **Refine $W(m,L,B)$ itself** — is the uniform box $\{0..B\}^m\cap\{\sum\le L\}$ optimal?
4. Whether bounded $B$ is necessary asymptotically.

## Crucial gap this exposes (links to Griego/G2026)
Zheng's framework ASSUMES the digit alphabet is the full uniform interval $\{0,1,\dots,B\}$
(that's where mean $=B/2$ and the uniform mgf come from). **Griego's record 1.1740744 uses a
NON-UNIFORM alphabet $A=\{0,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10\}$ (omits 1!) with global sum cap $T=150$,
base 21.** So the Zheng asymptotic formula does NOT cover Griego's set — generalizing the
rate function $I$ to an arbitrary digit-weight distribution is an untried, concrete lever.
