# Digest: Lemm, "New counterexamples for sums-differences" (arXiv:1404.3745, Proc. AMS 2015) [L2015]

## What the method is
Lemm computes lower bounds on the sum-difference / arithmetic-Kakeya constants via the
**entropy formulation**. For the 4-slope constant C_3c = SD({0,1,2,∞};-1), the entropy
form is: C_3c is the smallest C such that for all discrete random variables X,Y,
  H(X-Y) <= C * max( H(X), H(Y), H(X+Y), H(X+2Y) ).
A lower bound is obtained from any explicit joint law of (X,Y): the ratio
  α(P) = H(X-Y) / max( H(X), H(Y), H(X+Y), H(X+2Y) )
is a valid lower bound on C_3c. (Equivalently put a probability measure P on a finite
support G ⊂ Z^2 and push forward under the linear maps (a,b)↦a-b, a, b, a+b, a+2b.)

Key innovation over Ruzsa's earlier bounds: use **non-uniform** weights, not just
uniform measures on a structured set.

## Values it gets
- C_3b = SD({0,1,∞}): α > **1.77898** on a **7-point** support, non-uniform weights
  (p1≈.00024983, p2≈.028156, p3≈.22425, with the symmetry constraint H(π_0 P)=H(π_∞ P)).
- C_3c = SD({0,1,2,∞}): α > **1.61226** on a **5-point** support, weights
  p1=p2=p3=p4 ≈ .21798 (four equal masses + one different).

## Tensor / power argument (why the ratio is a genuine asymptotic lower bound)
Approximate P by rational weights k_g/M. Form the "type" set G' of all ordered M-tuples
in which point g∈G appears exactly k_g times. As M→∞, by Stirling, the cardinality ratio
|differences|/|max(images)| of G' converges to 2^{M·(numerator−denominator)}, i.e. the
exponent → α(P). Ruzsa's entropy equivalence (his Prop. 1.1) makes this rigorous: the
entropy ratio of a single finite (X,Y) is exactly the achievable SD exponent in the limit.

## Where the slack is
- Support is tiny (5 points for 3c). The constant is a sup over *all* finite supports;
  every later improvement came from **enlarging and reshaping the support**.
- The optimal weights are found by a small continuous optimization once the support
  (integer point set) is fixed — the combinatorial choice of support is the hard part.

## Verification note
A claimed lower bound is checked by evaluating α(P) for the explicit P to high precision
(ideally rational/interval arithmetic on the entropy logs). No conjecture is involved:
any feasible P with α(P) > record beats the record.
