# GHR2007 — Gyarmati, Hennecart, Ruzsa, "Sums and differences of finite sets" (2007)

PDF: `pdfs/GHR2007.pdf` (+ `.txt`). Funct. Approx. Comment. Math. 37(1):175-186.

## The constant and the framing
$C_{3a}$ ($=C_{6.44}$ in the AlphaEvolve paper) = sup of $\theta$ such that there are
arbitrarily large integer sets $A,B$ with $|A+B|\le K|A|$ and $|A-B|\ge c(K)|A+B|^\theta$.

## The load-bearing lemma (single-set route) — origin of (6.15)
For any finite $U\subset\mathbb{N}$ with $0\in U$ (and the technical constraint
$|U-U|\le 2\max U+1$),
$$ C_{3a}\ \ge\ 1+\frac{\log(|U-U|/|U+U|)}{\log(2\max U+1)}. $$
Proof idea: from one set $U$ build $A,B$ (a "diagonal/projection" construction) so that
$|A+B|\approx|U+U|$, $|A-B|\approx|U-U|$, $|A|\approx|U|$, and the exponent comes out as
$1+(\log d-\log s)/\log q$ with $d=|U-U|$, $s=|U+U|$, $q=2\max U+1$. (Eq. (11),(12) in
paper.) **This is the inequality every later construction (AlphaEvolve, Gerbicz, Zheng,
Griego) plugs a set $U$ into.**

## THE 1.25 CAP (why the single-set route stalls)
The paper proves (and the AlphaEvolve paper restates, p. ~4706): **the lower bound given by
(6.15) cannot exceed $5/4=1.25$**, no matter which $U$ you choose. So the entire
single-set / digit-construction family is fundamentally capped at 1.25. The record 1.1740744
is *inside* this regime — there is room up to 1.25 before this lemma is exhausted, then a
genuinely new idea (asymmetric $A\ne B$, products, a different inequality) is needed to push
toward the $4/3$ upper bound.

## The original constructions
- $V(m,L)=\{x\in\mathbb{N}^m: x_1+\dots+x_m\le L\}$, $|V(m,L)|=\binom{m+L}{m}$.
- Map to integers $f(x)=\sum_k x_k L_k$ with $L_0=1,\ L_k=2L\cdot L_{k-1}+1$ (ensures $f$
  injective on $V+V$ and $V-V$ — no carries). $U=f(V)$.
- Best clean value: $\theta=1.135596$. Then a relaxed $\Lambda=(L_k)$ (dropping
  $L_j> L\cdot L_{j-1}$, losing a few sums/diffs in projection) with $m=11,L=7$,
  $\Lambda=(1,15,211,1590,14976,109870,605315,3362489,17767138,80137194,408850463)$ gives
  $s(U)=4455634,\ d(U)=110205905,\ q(U)=5723906483 \Rightarrow \theta=1.144655$ = the
  [GHR2007] record.

## The 4/3 upper bound
Theorem 2 + Corollary 3: via Plünnecke / Ruzsa triangle, $|A+B|\le K|A|$ forces
$|A-B|\ll |A+B|^{4/3}$, so $\theta\le 4/3$. This is the hard analytic side (NOT our target;
we push the lower bound).

## Takeaways for attacking the lower bound
- The digit constructions live entirely inside (6.15); the ceiling for this whole family is
  **1.25**, so 1.1740744 → ~1.25 is the exploitable corridor before a new idea is needed.
- The free objects are: the set $U$ (equivalently a digit alphabet + a constraint region),
  subject only to $0\in U$ and injectivity of the integer encoding on $U\pm U$ (no carries).
