# G2025 — Gerbicz, "Sums and differences of sets (improvement over AlphaEvolve)" arXiv:2505.16105

PDF: `pdfs/2505.16105.pdf` (+ `.txt`). Result: $C_{3a}\ge 1.173050$.

## The construction (introduces the per-coordinate bound B + base-(2B+1) encoding)
Start from the box-truncated simplex
$$ W(m,L,B)=\{x\in\mathbb{N}^m:\ x_1+\dots+x_m\le L,\ \ x_i\le B\ \forall i\}. $$
(Note: the paper's eq (2.1) drops the `≤ L` in print, but the sum cap IS used — see (2.2);
Zheng restates it correctly.) Encode with **fixed base $2B+1$**:
$$ g(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{m-1} x_k(2B+1)^k. $$
$U=g(W)$. Because each coordinate of $W\pm W$ lies in $[-2B,2B]$ and base $=2B+1$, $g$ is
injective on $W+W$ and $W-W$ (no carries). **Key idea:** the GHR sequence $L_k\sim(2L)^k$
blows up $\max U$; bounding each $x_k\le B$ lets you use the much smaller base $(2B+1)^k$,
shrinking $\log q=\log(2\max U+1)$ (the denominator) without losing much in the numerator.

## Exact counting identities (all reused verbatim by Zheng and Griego)
- $|W(m,L,B)|=\sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor L/(B+1)\rfloor}(-1)^k\binom{m}{k}\binom{m+L-k(B+1)}{m}$
  (inclusion-exclusion on the $x_i\le B$ caps). (2.3)
- $s(U)=|U+U|=|W(m,2L,2B)|$. (2.4)
- $d(U)=|U-U|=\sum_{k=0}^{\min(m,L)}\binom{m}{k}\,|W(k,L-k,B-1)|\,|W(m-k,L,B)|$ (split by #
  positive coords). (2.5)
- $q(U)=2\max U+1=(2B+1)^m-(2B+1)^{m-t}+2(L\%B)(2B+1)^{m-t-1}+1$, $t=\lfloor L/B\rfloor$. (2.6)
- Then $\theta\ge 1+(\log d-\log s)/\log q$.

## The numbers and the optimization
- Empirical pattern: for fixed $L$, best $\theta$ near $B=5$, $m\approx \tfrac54 L$.
- Record: $m=81411,\ L=2^{16}=65536,\ B=5$ → $\theta=1.173050$. $|U|\approx 6.31\cdot10^{43546}$.
- Method: float search (GMP) for candidates, then exact integer arithmetic to confirm
  (alternating sum (2.3) is numerically unstable; compute exactly then round). ~15 h CPU.

## What the author says is open
- "Further improvements might be possible by increasing $L$, but any additional gain is likely
  < 0.0001." (i.e. **the uniform $\{0..B\}$ / single-cap family is essentially saturated at
  ~1.1731 — confirmed by Zheng's asymptotic 1.173077; Griego beat it by changing the
  ALPHABET, not by pushing $L$.**)

## Lever exposed
- Free parameters: $B$ (digit ceiling), $m$ (#digits), $L$ (sum cap). Discrete, exact-counting
  — Lean-fit. But this exact family is saturated; the gain came from generalizing $W$.
