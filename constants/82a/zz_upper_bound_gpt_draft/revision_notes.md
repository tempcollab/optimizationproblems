# Revision notes

This draft implements a submission-oriented restructuring of the original LaTeX paper.

Major changes made:

1. Retitled the paper to emphasize the main mathematical mechanism: first variations and block selection.
2. Rewrote the abstract to foreground the core contributions: the one-sided variation formula, the firing criterion, the certified improvement over Doche, the diagnostic for the current record, and the sibling generator.
3. Rebuilt the introduction around a clearer arc: problem, Doche functional, first-variation theorem, selection criterion, applications, generator, scope, and organization.
4. Added a formal admissibility definition in the functional section and separated it from Doche's upper-bound lemma.
5. Renamed major sections to make the paper easier to navigate and more transparent to referees.
6. Softened over-strong claims by distinguishing certified interval results from grid diagnostics and finite-search evidence.
7. Kept the mathematical content, numerical values, polynomial data, and bibliography essentially intact.
8. Fixed compilation issues, citations in displayed equations, overfull boxes, theorem-style ordering, and a confusing proposition label.

The revised draft compiles cleanly with pdflatex plus bibtex8 and has been rendered to PDF for a layout check.
